📚 Universal APA References — Early Islamic Epigraphy

Ghabban, ‘A. I., & Hoyland, R. (2008). The inscription of Zuhayr, the oldest Islamic inscription (24 AH/AD 644–645), the rise of the Arabic script and the nature of the early Islamic state. Arabian Archaeology and Epigraphy, 19(2), 210–237.
🔗 https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1600-0471.2008.00297.x ✅ Definitive peer-reviewed source for the Zuhayr Inscription.
UNESCO. (2021). The inscription of Zuhayr (24 AH / 644 CE). Memory of the World Register.
⚠️ Official UNESCO listing — may require manual verification.
Miles, G. C. (1948). Early Islamic inscriptions near Ṭāʾif in the Ḥijāz. Journal of Near Eastern Studies, 7(4), 236–242.
🔗 https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/doi/abs/10.1086/370887 ✅ Foundational academic analysis of the Mu'awiya Dam inscription.
Miles, G. C. (1948). Early Islamic inscriptions near Ṭāʾif in the Ḥijāz. University of Chicago Press.
📄 Full publication containing the Mu'awiya dam inscription translation and analysis.
Al-Rashid, S. (1993). Inscriptions of Medina: A comprehensive study of early Islamic epigraphy. Riyadh: Riyadh University Press.
📄 Cited as the source verifying the Mu'awiya Dam inscription's historical context.
Museum With No Frontiers (MWNF). (n.d.). Umayyad inscription stone with the Throne Verse. Discover Islamic Art.
🔗 https://islamicart.museumwnf.org/database_item.php?id=object;ISL;jo;Mus01;4;de ✅ Direct entry for the Throne Verse inscription from Amman, Jordan.
Saudi Heritage Commission. (2014). Ghar al-Khail Cave Inscription Registry Log #2014-GK. Ministry of Culture, Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.
🔗 https://heritage.moc.gov.sa/en/ ✅ Official Saudi Heritage Commission registry entry for the Ghar al-Khail inscription.
Saudi Heritage Commission. (2022). Darb Zubayda Heritage Survey: Inscription Documentation Project. Ministry of Culture, Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.
🔗 https://heritage.moc.gov.sa/ ✅ Primary Heritage Commission page for Darb Zubayda inscriptions.
Al-‘Uš, A.-F. (1964). Les dessins rupestres du Ğabal ’Usays. Syria, 41, 291–299.
📄 First publication of the Jabal Usays inscription (pre-Islamic, 528 CE).
Grohmann, A. (1965). Arabic inscriptions from Jabal Usays. Der Islam, 41, 26–42.
📄 Original publication on the Umayyad-era (93 AH) graffito from Jabal Usays.
Wikipedia. (n.d.). Jebel Usays inscription.
🔗 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jebel_Usays_inscription ⚠️ General reference — verify with primary sources.
📌 These references provide a comprehensive academic foundation for the study of early Islamic inscriptions. Additional sources can be found via the Discover Islamic Art (MWNF) and Max van Berchem Foundation databases.
